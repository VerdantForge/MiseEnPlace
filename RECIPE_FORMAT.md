# Example: Weeknight Chicken Stir-Fry
**Total Time:** 20 mins | **Yield:** 2 Servings

### 1. The Primer
* **Environment:** Start the rice cooker immediately. 
* **Mental Walkthrough:** Things move fast once the chicken is done. Make sure the sauce is fully mixed so I don't scramble looking for soy sauce while the garlic burns.

### 2. Gathering (The Pull)
**Hardware:**
* Kitchen scale
* 1 Wok or large skillet
* 3 prep bowls, 1 small plate
* Cutting board and chef's knife

**Ingredients:**
* Chicken breast, yellow onion, bell pepper, broccoli
* Garlic, ginger
* Soy sauce, sesame oil, cornstarch, chicken broth, cooking oil

### 3. Prepping the Bowls (Mise en Place)
* **Protein Board:** 450g chicken breast (sliced thin, tossed with 15g soy sauce and 5g cornstarch).
* **Bowl 1 (The Veggies):** 150g yellow onion (sliced), 150g bell pepper (sliced), 100g broccoli florets.
* **Bowl 2 (The Aromatics):** 15g garlic (minced), 15g fresh ginger (grated).
* **Bowl 3 (The Sauce):** *Tare scale between each addition ->* 60g chicken broth, 30g soy sauce, 15g sesame oil, 5g cornstarch (whisked together).

### 4. The Fire (Execution)
1. **Sear:** Wok on HIGH heat. Add 15g cooking oil. Cook the **Protein Board** for 4 minutes until browned. Remove to the small plate.
2. **Sauté Veggies:** Keep heat on HIGH. Add **Bowl 1** to the wok. Toss frequently for 3 minutes until slightly tender.
3. **Bloom Aromatics:** Lower heat to MEDIUM. Push veggies to the sides. Add a drop of oil to the center and drop in **Bowl 2**. Stir for 30 seconds until fragrant.
4. **The Finish:** Pour in **Bowl 3** and stir everything together. Return the chicken to the wok. Simmer for 1 minute until the sauce thickens and coats the food. Serve over rice.
